# AI Microscope - Privacy Policy

## 📋 Privacy and Data Protection Policy

**Effective Date**: March 9, 2026  
**Version**: 1.0.0  
**Institution**: Mbeya Regional Referral Hospital  

---

## 🎯 Purpose

This privacy policy outlines how AI Microscope handles, stores, and protects patient data and clinical information in accordance with medical data protection standards and Tanzanian healthcare regulations.

---

## 🔒 Data Collection & Use

### What Data We Collect

#### Clinical Data
- **Patient Identification**: Patient ID, medical record numbers
- **Diagnostic Results**: Bacterial species identification, confidence scores
- **Medical Images**: Microscope images and Grad-CAM heatmaps
- **Timestamps**: Date and time of analysis
- **Metadata**: Image parameters, camera settings, analysis conditions

#### Technical Data
- **Usage Logs**: Application usage patterns and performance metrics
- **Error Logs**: System errors and troubleshooting information
- **Configuration Data**: User settings and preferences
- **System Information**: Hardware specifications and performance data

### How We Use Data

#### Primary Clinical Use
- **Patient Diagnosis**: Assist healthcare professionals in bacterial identification
- **Medical Records**: Store results in patient clinical files
- **Quality Assurance**: Monitor system accuracy and reliability
- **Clinical Research**: Anonymized data for medical research (with consent)

#### System Operations
- **Service Improvement**: Enhance AI model accuracy and performance
- **Technical Support**: Diagnose and resolve technical issues
- **Security Monitoring**: Detect and prevent unauthorized access
- **Compliance**: Ensure regulatory compliance and auditing

---

## 🛡️ Data Protection Measures

### Security Safeguards

#### Technical Protection
- **Local Storage**: All data stored locally on hospital servers
- **Encryption**: Database encryption for sensitive information
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access to patient data
- **Audit Logging**: Complete audit trail of all data access
- **Network Security**: Firewall protection and intrusion detection

#### Physical Security
- **Server Security**: Secure server room with restricted access
- **Backup Security**: Encrypted backups stored in secure locations
- **Device Security**: Medical-grade equipment and secure workstations
- **Environmental Controls**: Climate-controlled server environment

### Data Integrity
- **Validation**: Input validation and data integrity checks
- **Backup Systems**: Regular automated backups with verification
- **Version Control**: Track all changes to patient data
- **Recovery Procedures**: Disaster recovery and business continuity plans

## 👥 Access Rights & Responsibilities

### Authorized Access
- **Healthcare Professionals**: Direct patient care providers
- **Laboratory Staff**: Microscopy and diagnostic personnel
- **System Administrators**: IT support and maintenance staff
- **Researchers**: Authorized research personnel (with ethics approval)

### Access Levels
- **Full Access**: Primary care physicians for their patients
- **Limited Access**: Specialists with specific patient consent
- **Administrative Access**: System administrators for maintenance
- **Read-Only Access**: Quality assurance and auditing personnel

### User Responsibilities
- **Authentication**: Use secure login credentials
- **Confidentiality**: Maintain patient confidentiality
- **Proper Use**: Use system only for legitimate medical purposes
- **Reporting**: Report security incidents immediately

## 📊 Data Retention & Disposal

### Retention Policy
- **Clinical Data**: Retained according to medical record requirements (minimum 7 years)
- **Images**: Stored as part of patient medical records
- **Audit Logs**: Retained for security and compliance (3 years)
- **System Data**: Retained for operational needs (1 year)

### Disposal Procedures
- **Secure Deletion**: Cryptographic wiping of digital data
- **Physical Destruction**: Secure disposal of physical media
- **Documentation**: Record of all data disposal activities
- **Verification**: Confirmation of secure data destruction

## 🌐 Data Sharing & Disclosure

#### Permitted Sharing
- **Patient Care**: Sharing with other healthcare providers for treatment
- **Emergency Situations**: When required for immediate medical care
- **Legal Requirements**: When required by law or court order
- **Public Health**: Disease reporting to health authorities
- **Research**: Anonymized data with proper ethics approval

#### Prohibited Sharing
- **Marketing**: Never used for commercial marketing
- **Sale**: Never sold to third parties
- **Unnecessary Disclosure**: No sharing beyond medical necessity
- **International Transfer**: No cross-border data without specific consent

#### Data Sharing Procedures
- **Minimum Necessary**: Share only essential information
- **Patient Consent**: Obtain explicit consent when required
- **Secure Transmission**: Encrypted transfer methods
- **Documentation**: Record all data sharing activities
- **Access Agreements**: Data use agreements with recipients
